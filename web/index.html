This is MacFlim Player, the Macintosh flim player. Whichever Macintosh you have, if it has one of those great 68000 CPUs you will be able to admire and listen to many flim clips, in their glorious black and white.

If your Macintosh is currently getting re-capped or if you are unfortunately stuck in the past with an IBM 5150, you can still enjoy the flims by clicking on the animated gifs to play them in your browser.

  • Supported systems

    MacFlim Player supports all current Macs, from the 128K to the SE/30, including the Mac XL, or the new Macintosh Portable.

  • Just double-click on one or several flims, and the fun starts!

The new version of MacFlim has arrived!

Remember when your kids uttered their first words? What a milestone! Well, the same happened to MacFlim, it has now learned how to speak! And can play Flims at their exact orginal frame rate! And thanks to is new lossy compression, flims size are 3 to 4 times smaller than first version of MacFlim! And the encoder is way simpler to use, so creating your own flims is a breeze!

disclaimer: compressed flims are not always as nice (due to the extra space used for sound), the player doesn't support multiple windows, and have slightly less inside jokes, but we are working hard on that.

So, what are you waiting for?

How to install MacFlim Player

Beware, flim files can still be quite big. How big? Well, it depends on the desired quality. You are looking at around 100Kb/seconds for Mac Plus quality, 150 for SE and more than 300 for SE30.

You need a SCSI2SD (if you can make sense of the different models), or a RaSCSI (if you like soldering), or a BlueSCSI (if you don't live in the EU) to store them. There is also the MacSD which I know nothing about, but it does look neat.

Download the app, as a .dsk file

#### PUT THE RELEASE VERSION HERE #### FOR NOW GRAB THE APP FROM THE DSK IN https://www.github.com/fstark/macflim

Flims are available as ``.flim`` files. Just download the ones you want, move them to your mac and add them to you MacFlim Library

While you can play a flim for a Mac Plus on a SE or a SE/30, or play a SE flim on a SE/30, the reverse is not possible, so download the best possible flim for your hardware

By clicking on the animated gif, you will directly plays an mp4 of the resulting flim. However, as mp4 is not designed to compress well high frequency black and white, those files are *much larger* than the flims (like between x10 and x25x), they can easily bring the MacFlim server down

#### MOVE TO THE END

If you like the idea of creating your own flims, you can install flimmaker on linux or mac os x. You can also use flimmaker to directly generate mp4 files that shows the frame perfect result of MacFlim on selected hardware, so you don't need to have access to a vintage mac to enjoy it[1] .
